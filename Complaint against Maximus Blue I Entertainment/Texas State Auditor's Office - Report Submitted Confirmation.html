Fraud Description

I'm reporting an anomaly: widespread organized criminal financial fraud exploiting Victoria, TX residents via Visa cards. My bank's specialist confirmed this affects various local businesses, including Donut Palace (3602 Houston Hwy # J, Victoria, TX 77901). Despite local police (VPD) dismissing it as isolated, it affects many. On 6/6/2025, at 'Maximus' (1901 N Ben Wilson St, Victoria, TX, d/b/a 'Blue I Entertainment'), after paying a 21$ transaction for 3 drinks). Staff immediately took my receipt, and then I overheard them discussing forging tips. On 6/8/2025, my card was charged $32.83 (an unauthorized $10 tip) after a suspicious 2-day delay. As I left, the bouncer threatened to charge me $40 next time if I didn't tip. This incident, by 'Blue I Entertainment'/'Maximus,' is intentional fraud. Identified crimes/violations include debit card fraud, forgery, theft by deception, extortion, DTPA violation, and implied conspiracy. Indications of organized crime: systematic operation (overheard discussion), intentional evidence concealment (receipt taken), delayed processing, intimidation, targeting patrons, and discrepant business names. My TDECU specialist also confirmed this regional fraud pattern, leading to my fraud affidavit denial despite the EMV chip. Local authorities' reluctance to investigate widespread exploitation is concerning. I urge thorough investigations into 'Blue I Entertainment'/'Maximus' and this pervasive organized criminal financial fraud in Victoria. A dedicated local system is crucial to identify this phenomenon, as current approaches fail to capture its true scale. We need more analysis, indications, and public awareness about businesses overcharging/adding tips without consent.
